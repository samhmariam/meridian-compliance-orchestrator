# REACH Regulation — Annex XVII Restricted Substances (Guidance R1, 2024)

This document provides authoritative guidance on restrictions applicable under
Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006 (REACH), Annex XVII, as consolidated through
January 2024.  It is the Tier 1 authoritative source for chemical compliance
determinations within the Meridian qualification workflow.

## Scope and Applicability

This guidance applies to all substances placed on the EU market by manufacturers,
importers, and downstream users established within the European Economic Area
(EEA).  It does not extend to goods placed exclusively on the GB market after
31 December 2020; for GB-specific obligations refer to UK REACH (GB regulation
SI 2019/758).

Suppliers subject to EEA distribution must ensure that all articles and
preparations comply with the concentration limits specified per Annex XVII entry
before requesting ERP activation.

## Annex XVII — Entry 28: Lead and Lead Compounds

### Scope

Lead (CAS No 7439-92-1) and lead compounds shall not be used or placed on the
market as substances or as constituents of preparations if the concentration of
lead is equal to or greater than **0.03% by weight**.

This restriction applies to:
- articles intended to be used by the general public, and
- articles in contact with children under 36 months.

### Exemptions

The following article categories are exempted from Entry 28 obligations, subject
to the conditions stated in each sub-entry:

- Crystal glass as defined in Annex I to Directive 69/493/EEC.
- Enamels for vitreous and porcelain articles.
- Ammunition, until superseded by Entry 63 provisions.

### Enforcement Thresholds

| Parameter | Value | Unit |
|---|---|---|
| Maximum concentration | 0.03 | % by weight |
| Test method reference | EN 71-3:2019+A1:2021 | — |
| Age applicability | Under 36 months | — |

## Annex XVII — Entry 63: Cadmium and Cadmium Compounds

### Scope

Cadmium (CAS No 7440-43-9) and cadmium compounds shall not be used in plastic
articles or in brazing alloys when the concentration of cadmium is equal to or
greater than **0.01% by weight**.

This entry was amended by Commission Regulation (EU) 2021/57, which tightened
the threshold from 0.1% to 0.01% effective 30 April 2021.

### Transition Provisions

Stock manufactured before 30 April 2021 at the legacy 0.1% threshold may be
sold until 30 April 2022 under the grandfathering clause.  Inventory held
after that date is non-compliant regardless of manufacture date.
